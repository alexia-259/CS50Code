So... What is a Spaced Repetition System?

Great question! It's a way of learning information more effectively via spaced out repetition. You first do a lesson, then a review. After some time has passed, you can come again and do another review. The reviews will be spread out more and more, so your brain has time to think and understand what its learning. Because of the repetition, you will slowly get the, in this context, hiragana faster than any other way! If you get something fast, great! You won't need to do it over and over again, as you already know it. However, if something is more tricky, we will show it to you again, to make sure you understand.
